Day Trip

08:00 · 0m

Departure from Seoul

Start your day trip to the DMZ from a designated meeting point in Seoul. It is recommended to join a guided tour for DMZ visits due to security regulations and specific entry procedures.

09:30 · 1h

Imjingak Park

The first stop near the DMZ, Imjingak Park is home to the Bridge of Freedom, which was used by repatriated POWs and soldiers, and the Mangbaedan Altar, where Koreans whose hometowns are in the North can pay their respects to their ancestors. Explore the various monuments and displays reflecting the pain of division and the hope for reunification.

11:00 · 1h 30m

The Third Tunnel of Aggression

Discovered in 1978, this tunnel was built by North Korea for a surprise attack on Seoul. Visitors can walk or take a monorail into a portion of the tunnel, offering a stark reminder of the ongoing tensions and the ingenuity of both sides during the Cold War.

13:00 · 1h

Dora Observatory

From this observation point, you can gaze across the DMZ into North Korean territory, seeing the city of Kaesong, the Kaesong Industrial Complex, and propaganda villages. Binoculars are available to get a closer look at the secluded land.

14:30 · 45m

Dorasan Station

The northernmost railway station in South Korea, Dorasan Station symbolizes the hope for reunification. It is intended to connect to North Korean railways in the future, allowing travel from South Korea through North Korea to Europe. Explore the station and its poignant history.
